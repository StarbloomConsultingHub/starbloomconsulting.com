# LinkedIn Post 3 — "Why I'm Not a Tech Founder"
## Publish: Week 3 | Tone: Positioning, origin story

---

I didn't come from Silicon Valley. I didn't do a Y Combinator batch. I've never pitched a VC.

I came from construction sites. Erosion control crews. Behavior intervention for vulnerable populations. Then seven years running a large-scale production facility — working my way from flower room labor to Director of Cultivation, designing every SOP from scratch.

When I started building with AI, I did it the same way I learned everything else: hands-on, on my own hardware, because I didn't trust anyone else's cloud with my data.

That turned into a full platform. Multi-node Edge AI orchestration clusters running Docker, n8n, Ollama, and Open WebUI. Outbound-only Cloudflare Tunnels — zero inbound firewall changes. Vision-RPA agents scraping data out of closed-source legacy ERPs. 24/7 conversational AI intake engines that answer, qualify, and book jobs while crews sleep. Google BigQuery pipelines streaming from on-premise systems to live Looker Studio dashboards.

I didn't build this because I wanted to be in tech. I built it because the industries I come from are drowning in data they can't access — locked inside desktop software from 2003, voicemail boxes, paper logs. And the "solution" the AI industry offers is "upload everything to our cloud and pay per token forever."

No thanks. Build local. Own the stack. Control the data.

Starbloom Consulting LLC isn't a SaaS company. It's an Edge AI & Automation Orchestration Platform for operators who build things that matter. If you're running a real business with real crews and real deadlines, I speak your language.

The Maryland Leaf profile from their November 2025 issue tells the full career arc if you want the backstory. The rest of the platform is at starbloomconsulting.com.
